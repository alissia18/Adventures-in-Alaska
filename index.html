Adventures in Alaska

British Columbia, CanadaVancouver

Vancouver

We arrived in Vancouver around late morning, leaving us the whole afternoon to explore the city. After a brief stop at the hotel room, we took a walk along the boardwalk. It was an unusually warm day, luckily for us. The clear blue of the sky that day reflected almost perfectly in the water. The backdrop of the Rocky mountains created a stunning, unforgettable scene.

×

We spent the rest of our day wandering through the streets of Vancouver surrounding its port, Canada Place. The following afternoon, though, it was time to board our cruise ship to begin our journey to Alaska. As the ship left its dock, we bore witness to incredible views of the city we had departed from. We watched the skyline grow smaller until it was no longer visible. At last, we were in open waters, on our way to the Alaskan Inside Passage.

Alaska, USADawes Glacier

Dawes Glacier

After two leisurely days at sea, in the morning on the third day of our trip, we began to get our first glimpses of the fjord. Though we were still hours away from Dawes Glacier itself, the scenery of the Inside Passage was stunning. We were surrounded by clear turquoise waters dotted with floating ice blocks. With the mist-covered mountains and lush green forests, it was as if we had stepped into a painting. Of course, as we travelled up north, it got quite cold, and the wind was biting. However, it was a small price to pay for the breathtaking views we got that morning as we approached the glacier.

Alaska, USASkagway

Skagway

At last, the following morning, the ship docked in Skagway, Alaska. This is by far the smallest city I have ever seen. There is only one major street – it is called Broadway. The most spectacular thing about Skagway is that this small town’s picturesque buildings are surrounded by mountains. As if the town was not already pleasant enough, we happened to get lucky because it was so warm that day that I did not need a jacket outside. The skies were perfectly clear, and the sun’s warming rays made for perfect weather.

We visited some of the shops on the main road, but after walking through we decided to stray from the path. Not too far from the main road, surrounded by some trees, we stumbled upon Skagway Museum. It was quaint and not too crowded, so we decided to step inside and take a look. I am glad we did – it was filled with lovely art and informative displays. It even featured antique furniture and room mock-ups, offering a truly lifelike glimpse into the past.

We still had some time after our visit inside the museum, so we opted for a walk to the nearby Pullen Creek Park. It was a serene area with some very scenic views. We spent some time admiring the surroundings here before it was time to head back to the ship for departure. Our next stop would be Juneau, the capital of Alaska.

Alaska, USAJuneau

Juneau

In Juneau, we had opted for an excursion which began with a visit to the Mendenhall Glacier. I could not believe how pretty it was! When we had visited the Dawes Glacier it was surrounded by cold, barren landscape. By contrast, the Mendenhall Glacier was bordered by beautiful greenery and even some flowers! It was fascinating to follow the observation trail and see the array of plant life there. Best of all, though, was the picturesque view from the top of the observation trail. It was an unforgettable moment. Sadly, we learned that the glacier had once been much more massive and impressive. Due to climate change, it has receded over the years. Still, I found our view of the glacier awe-inspiring.

After visiting the glacier itself, we got to tour the glacier gardens. Here, we saw some upside-down trees: Trees with the trunk sticking out of the ground, and flowers planted on top of the trunks. This was not a natural phenomenon. The owner of the garden explained that one day he’d backed into a log while driving. He was so angry that he threw the log into the pond, expecting it to shatter. Instead, it stayed there, stuck in the mud. So, he cut off the roots, laid down soil and planted various plants there. Thus, the first upside-down tree was created.

Up next was a golf cart ride up into Tongass national rainforest. Riding through the hilly terrain and into the trees was breathtaking. At 500-foot elevation, we began to see clouds all around us, even riding through some! This was an otherworldly experience. At the end of the trail, we stopped and took some time to admire the gorgeous panoramic view of the city far below. After that it was time to descend the rainforest back to ground level. This was highly exciting, and at some points I thought we might drive off the road but, luckily, we made it back down!

Our final stop was the salmon hatchery. Here we watched salmon jump along the stream, which was quite entertaining. It was extremely difficult to catch these small fish on camera. The hatchery was situated on the waterfront, and its bright red roof coupled with the deep waters made for a beautiful panoramic view. After this visit, we headed back to the ship for the night. The following morning we would be arriving in Ketchikan, our final port of call.

Alaska, USAKetchikan

Ketchikan

When we got off the ship in Ketchikan, we found a small general store which had maps of a walking tour that were free to take. We picked one up and followed along some of the tour spots listed. We didn’t have time to go through the entire tour but we did get some very nice views of the city. We took a break from following the tour to have a walk along the water, which was very peaceful. Although, it being Alaska, it was quite cold.

After our walk along the harbor, we stopped for lunch at a small restaurant called Annabelle’s. I had shrimp chowder, a dish I had never heard of before that day. I was pleasantly surprised; it was delicious. Unfortunately, we did not have very much time in Ketchikan, since we had to sail out early to be able to get back to Vancouver in time. So, after exploring the city a little more, our trip had come to a close. All in all, it was an unforgettable Alaskan adventure!
